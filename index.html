The elephant in the room

Over 80% of the grid‚Äôs climate impact in 2023 came from conducting equipment ‚Äî mainly overhead lines (47.6%) and cables (35.9%). Aluminium is the main culprit, with its high upstream electricity demand.

Masts also matter

About a third of overhead line impacts come from masts, mainly due to iron and steel production. These emissions are largely direct (from blast furnaces), but electricity matters too.

The single largest contributor

Among background processes, electricity generation is the top emitter (36.6%), followed by heat supply (11%) and iron & steel production (10%). These sectors drive most of the upstream emissions.

Takeaways

Aluminium is key to reducing grid emissions. Its high climate impact and dominance in cables and overhead lines make it the top hotspot. Swapping materials or greening upstream electricity could help.
